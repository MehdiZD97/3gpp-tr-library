# 7.5 Fast fading model

Clause 7.5 defines the step-by-step procedure for generating a full small-scale ("fast fading") channel realization between a BS and a UT, on top of the large-scale pathloss and LOS-probability results from §7.4. The procedure is geometry-based: it generates cluster and ray departure/arrival angles, delays, and powers, then combines them with antenna field patterns to produce a time-varying channel impulse response. Downlink generation is assumed throughout; uplink can reuse the same impulse response under an ideal-reciprocity assumption. The TR notes this procedure alone (without any of clause 7.6's additional modelling components — spatial consistency, large bandwidth/array corrections, oxygen absorption, blockage, etc.) already covers low-complexity evaluations, comparisons against earlier simulation results, and any case where none of clause 7.6's components are enabled.

Table 7.5-1 defines the notation used throughout the procedure (LOS/cluster/ray angles, antenna field patterns):

| Parameter | Notation |
|---|---|
| LOS AOD | $\phi_{LOS,AOD}$ |
| LOS AOA | $\phi_{LOS,AOA}$ |
| LOS ZOD | $\theta_{LOS,ZOD}$ |
| LOS ZOA | $\theta_{LOS,ZOA}$ |
| AOA for cluster n | $\phi_{n,AOA}$ |
| AOD for cluster n | $\phi_{n,AOD}$ |
| AOA for ray m in cluster n | $\phi_{n,m,AOA}$ |
| AOD for ray m in cluster n | $\phi_{n,m,AOD}$ |
| ZOA for cluster n | $\theta_{n,ZOA}$ |
| ZOD for cluster n | $\theta_{n,ZOD}$ |
| ZOA for ray m in cluster n | $\theta_{n,m,ZOA}$ |
| ZOD for ray m in cluster n | $\theta_{n,m,ZOD}$ |
| Receive antenna element $u$ field pattern ($\hat\theta$ direction) | $F_{rx,u,\theta}$ |

## Steps 1-4: environment, propagation condition, pathloss, large-scale parameters

**Step 1** sets up the scenario: choose a deployment scenario (UMa, UMi-Street Canyon, RMa — up to 7 GHz only, InH-Office, or InF) and a global coordinate system; specify BS/UT counts, 3D locations (which fix the LOS AOD/ZOD/AOA/ZOA angles), antenna field patterns and array geometries, BS/UT orientations (each defined by a bearing, downtilt, and slant angle), UT motion (speed and direction), and the system centre frequency and bandwidth. Each wrapped copy of a BS/site under wraparound is treated as a separate BS/site for this generation.

**Step 2** assigns each BS-UT link's propagation condition (LOS/NLOS) using the LOS-probability formulas of Table 7.4.2-1 (§7.4.2), and an indoor/outdoor state per UT (shared across all of that UT's links). Propagation conditions across different BS-UT links are uncorrelated.

**Step 3** calculates pathloss for every BS-UT link using the formulas of Table 7.4.1-1 (§7.4.1).

**Step 4** generates the large-scale parameters (LSPs) — delay spread (DS), angular spreads (ASA, ASD, ZSA, ZSD), Ricean K-factor (K), and shadow fading (SF) — taking cross-correlation into account per Table 7.5-6, using the procedure of clause 3.3.1 of [14]: the square-root correlation matrix is generated via Cholesky decomposition, with the LSP vector ordered $[s_{SF}, s_K, s_{DS}, s_{ASD}, s_{ASA}, s_{ZSD}, s_{ZSA}]^T$. LSPs are uncorrelated across different BS-UT links, except that links from co-sited sectors to the same UT share identical LSPs, and links to UTs on different floors are uncorrelated. Random RMS azimuth spreads (ASA, ASD) are capped at 104°, and random RMS zenith spreads (ZSA, ZSD) at 52°.

## Steps 5-6: cluster delays and powers

**Step 5** draws cluster delays from the delay distribution in Table 7.5-6. With an exponential delay distribution:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-1 -->
$$\tau_n' = -r_\tau D_S \ln(X_n)$$

<!-- Eq. 7.5-2 -->
$$\tau_n = \text{sort}(\tau_n' - \min(\tau_n'))$$

where $r_\tau$ is the delay distribution proportionality factor, $X_n \sim \text{uniform}(0,1)$, and cluster index $n = 1,\dots,N$. (With a uniform delay distribution, delay values are instead drawn directly from the applicable range.) Delays are normalized by subtracting the minimum and sorting ascending. In the LOS case, an additional heuristic scaling compensates for the LOS peak's effect on the delay spread:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-3 -->
$$C_\tau = 0.7705 - 0.0433K + 0.0002K^2 + 0.000017K^3$$

<!-- Eq. 7.5-4 -->
$$\tau_{n,LOS} = \tau_n / C_\tau$$

where $K$ [dB] is the Ricean K-factor from Step 4. These LOS-scaled delays are not used for cluster power generation.

**Step 6** computes cluster powers assuming a single-slope exponential power delay profile; power assignment depends on the delay distribution of Table 7.5-6. With an exponential delay distribution:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-5 -->
$$P_n' = \exp\left(-\tau_n \frac{r_\tau - 1}{r_\tau D_S}\right)\cdot 10^{-Z_n/10}$$

where $Z_n \sim N(0, \zeta^2)$ is the per-cluster shadowing term [dB]. Powers are then normalized to sum to one:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-6 -->
$$P_n = P_n' / \sum_{n=1}^{N} P_n'$$

In the LOS case, an additional specular component is added to the first cluster. The single LOS ray's power is:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-7 -->
$$P_{1,LOS} = \frac{K_R}{K_R+1}$$

and cluster powers are instead:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-8 -->
$$P_n = \frac{1}{K_R+1}\frac{P_n'}{\sum_{n=1}^N P_n'} + \delta(n-1)P_{1,LOS}$$

where $\delta(\cdot)$ is the Dirac delta function and $K_R$ is the Step-4 Ricean K-factor in linear scale; these LOS power values feed only into Eq. 7.5-9 and 7.5-14, not Eq. 7.5-22. Each ray within a cluster is assigned power $P_n/M$ ($M$ = rays per cluster). Clusters more than 25 dB below the strongest cluster (by Eq. 7.5-6) are removed; scaling factors are not recomputed after removal.

## Step 7: arrival and departure angles (azimuth and elevation)

The composite azimuth power-angle spectrum is modelled as wrapped Gaussian. AOAs come from applying the inverse Gaussian function to cluster powers and RMS angular spread ASA:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-9 -->
$$\phi_{n,AOA}' = \frac{2(ASA/1.4)\sqrt{-\ln(P_n/\max(P_n))}}{C_\phi}$$

with the scaling constant $C_\phi$:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-10 -->
$$C_\phi = \begin{cases} C_\phi^{NLOS}\left(1.1035 - 0.028K - 0.002K^2 + 0.0001K^3\right) & \text{LOS} \\ C_\phi^{NLOS} & \text{NLOS} \end{cases}$$

where $C_\phi^{NLOS}$ is a scaling factor depending on the total number of clusters (Table 7.5-2):

Random sign and Gaussian jitter are then applied:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-11 -->
$$\phi_{n,AOA} = X_n\phi_{n,AOA}' + Y_n + \phi_{LOS,AOA}$$

with $X_n$ uniform on $\{1,-1\}$ and $Y_n \sim N(0,(ASA/7)^2)$; $\phi_{LOS,AOA}$ is the Step-1c LOS direction. In the LOS case, Eq. 7.5-11 is replaced so the first cluster is forced exactly to the LOS direction:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-12 -->
$$\phi_{n,AOA} = (X_n\phi_{n,AOA}' + Y_n) - (X_1\phi_{1,AOA}' + Y_1 - \phi_{LOS,AOA})$$

Per-ray offsets $\alpha_m$ (Table 7.5-3) are then added:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-13 -->
$$\phi_{n,m,AOA} = \phi_{n,AOA} + c_{ASA}\alpha_m$$

where $c_{ASA}$ is the cluster-wise RMS azimuth spread of arrival angles (Table 7.5-6):

AOD generation ($\phi_{n,m,AOD}$) follows the identical procedure. ZOA generation assumes a Laplacian (not Gaussian) composite zenith power-angle spectrum:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-14 -->
$$\theta_{n,ZOA}' = -\frac{ZSA \ln(P_n/\max(P_n))}{C_\theta}$$

<!-- Eq. 7.5-15 -->
$$C_\theta = \begin{cases} C_\theta^{NLOS}\left(1.3086 + 0.0339K - 0.0077K^2 + 0.0002K^3\right) & \text{LOS} \\ C_\theta^{NLOS} & \text{NLOS} \end{cases}$$

with $C_\theta^{NLOS}$ from Table 7.5-4 (same cluster-count keying as Table 7.5-2):

<!-- Eq. 7.5-16 -->
$$\theta_{n,ZOA} = X_n\theta_{n,ZOA}' + Y_n + \bar\theta_{ZOA}$$

with $X_n$ uniform on $\{1,-1\}$, $Y_n \sim N(0,(ZSA/7)^2)$, and $\bar\theta_{ZOA} = 90°$ for an O2I link or $\theta_{LOS,ZOA}$ otherwise. In the LOS case, the first cluster is again forced to the LOS direction:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-17 -->
$$\theta_{n,ZOA} = (X_n\theta_{n,ZOA}' + Y_n) - (X_1\theta_{1,ZOA}' + Y_1 - \theta_{LOS,ZOA})$$

<!-- Eq. 7.5-18 -->
$$\theta_{n,m,ZOA} = \theta_{n,ZOA} + c_{ZSA}\alpha_m$$

with $\theta_{n,m,ZOA}$ wrapped to $[0,360°]$ (replaced by $360° - \theta_{n,m,ZOA}$ if it falls in $[180°,360°]$).

ZOD generation follows the same procedure, with Eq. 7.5-16 replaced by:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-19 -->
$$\theta_{n,ZOD} = X_n\theta_{n,ZOD}' + Y_n + \theta_{LOS,ZOD} + \mu_{offset,ZOD}$$

($X_n$ uniform on $\{1,-1\}$, $Y_n \sim N(0,(ZSD/7)^2)$, $\mu_{offset,ZOD}$ from Tables 7.5-7 through 7.5-12), and Eq. 7.5-18 replaced by:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-20 -->
$$\theta_{n,m,ZOD} = \theta_{n,ZOD} + \frac{3}{8}\left(10^{\mu_{lgZSD}}\right)\alpha_m$$

where $\mu_{lgZSD}$ is the mean of the ZSD log-normal distribution. In the LOS case, ZOD generation instead reuses Eq. 7.5-17.

## Step 8: ray coupling

AOD angles are randomly coupled to AOA angles within each cluster (or within a sub-cluster, for the two strongest clusters — see Step 11 and Table 7.5-5); ZOD is coupled to ZOA the same way; and AOD is coupled to ZOD, all independently per cluster/sub-cluster.

## Step 9: cross-polarization power ratios

Cross-polarization power ratio (XPR) $\kappa$ is drawn per ray $m$ of cluster $n$, log-normally distributed:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-21 -->
$$\kappa_{n,m} = 10^{X_{n,m}/10}$$

where $X_{n,m} \sim N(\mu_{XPR}, \sigma_{XPR}^2)$ (independently drawn per ray and cluster), with $\mu_{XPR}$/$\sigma_{XPR}$ from Table 7.5-6. The outcome of Steps 1-9 is identical for all links from co-sited sectors to a UT.

## Steps 10-11: initial phases and channel coefficients

**Step 10** draws a random initial phase $\{\Phi_{n,m}^{\theta\theta}, \Phi_{n,m}^{\theta\phi}, \Phi_{n,m}^{\phi\theta}, \Phi_{n,m}^{\phi\phi}\}$, uniform on $(-\pi,\pi)$, per ray/cluster and per polarization combination.

**Step 11** generates channel coefficients per cluster and per Rx/Tx element pair $(u,s)$. The method below applies at least for drop-based evaluations (low-complexity evaluations; comparison with earlier results; when none of clause 7.6's components are active; when spatial consistency and/or blockage is modelled for MU-MIMO). For the $N-2$ weakest clusters ($n = 3,4,\dots,N$):

<!-- Eq. 7.5-22 -->
$$H_{u,s,n}^{NLOS}(t) = \sqrt{\frac{P_n}{M}}\sum_{m=1}^{M}
\begin{bmatrix} F_{rx,u,\theta}(\theta_{n,m,ZOA},\phi_{n,m,AOA}) \\ F_{rx,u,\phi}(\theta_{n,m,ZOA},\phi_{n,m,AOA}) \end{bmatrix}^T
\begin{bmatrix} \exp(j\Phi_{n,m}^{\theta\theta}) & \sqrt{\kappa_{n,m}^{-1}}\exp(j\Phi_{n,m}^{\theta\phi}) \\ \sqrt{\kappa_{n,m}^{-1}}\exp(j\Phi_{n,m}^{\phi\theta}) & \exp(j\Phi_{n,m}^{\phi\phi}) \end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix} F_{tx,s,\theta}(\theta_{n,m,ZOD},\phi_{n,m,AOD}) \\ F_{tx,s,\phi}(\theta_{n,m,ZOD},\phi_{n,m,AOD}) \end{bmatrix}
\exp\left(\frac{j2\pi(\hat r_{rx,n,m}^T \cdot \bar d_{rx,u})}{\lambda_0}\right)
\exp\left(\frac{j2\pi(\hat r_{tx,n,m}^T \cdot \bar d_{tx,s})}{\lambda_0}\right)
\exp\left(j2\pi\frac{\hat r_{rx,n,m}^T \cdot \bar v}{\lambda_0}t\right)$$

where $F_{rx,\cdot}$/$F_{tx,\cdot}$ are the Rx/Tx element field patterns (per clause 7.1, in the global coordinate system, including antenna-orientation transforms per clause 7.1), $\hat r_{rx,n,m}$/$\hat r_{tx,n,m}$ are spherical unit vectors toward the arrival/departure directions:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-23 -->
$$\hat r_{rx,n,m} = \begin{bmatrix} \sin\theta_{n,m,ZOA}\cos\phi_{n,m,AOA} \\ \sin\theta_{n,m,ZOA}\sin\phi_{n,m,AOA} \\ \cos\theta_{n,m,ZOA} \end{bmatrix}$$

<!-- Eq. 7.5-24 -->
$$\hat r_{tx,n,m} = \begin{bmatrix} \sin\theta_{n,m,ZOD}\cos\phi_{n,m,AOD} \\ \sin\theta_{n,m,ZOD}\sin\phi_{n,m,AOD} \\ \cos\theta_{n,m,ZOD} \end{bmatrix}$$

$\bar d_{rx,u}$/$\bar d_{tx,s}$ are Rx/Tx element location vectors, $\kappa_{n,m}$ is the linear-scale XPR, and $\lambda_0$ is the carrier wavelength. (Without polarization, the 2x2 matrix collapses to the scalar $\exp(j\Phi_{n,m})$ with only vertically-polarized field patterns applied.) The Doppler term depends on arrival angles and the UT velocity vector $\bar v$ (speed $v$, azimuth $\phi_v$, elevation $\theta_v$):

<!-- Eq. 7.5-25 -->
$$\nu_{n,m} = \frac{\hat r_{rx,n,m}^T \cdot \bar v}{\lambda_0}, \quad \bar v = v\cdot[\sin\theta_v\cos\phi_v \;\; \sin\theta_v\sin\phi_v \;\; \cos\theta_v]^T$$

For the **two strongest clusters** ($n=1,2$), rays are instead spread across three sub-clusters per cluster with fixed delay offsets (Table 7.5-5):

<!-- Eq. 7.5-26 -->
$$\tau_{n,1} = \tau_n, \quad \tau_{n,2} = \tau_n + 1.28\,c_{DS}, \quad \tau_{n,3} = \tau_n + 2.56\,c_{DS}$$

where $c_{DS}$ is the cluster delay spread from Table 7.5-6 (3.91 ns when unspecified there — the value that reproduces the legacy 5 ns/10 ns sub-cluster delays). The 20 rays of a cluster map to sub-clusters, with offset angles taken from Table 7.5-3 per that mapping:

_A matching CSV lives at `tables/table-7.5-5.csv`; YAML key `sub_cluster_info`._

| Sub-cluster $i$ | Mapping to rays $R_i$ | Power $\lvert R_i\rvert/M$ | Delay offset |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | {1,2,3,4,5,6,7,8,19,20} | 10/20 | $0$ |
| 2 | {9,10,11,12,17,18} | 6/20 | $1.28\, c_{DS}$ |
| 3 | {13,14,15,16} | 4/20 | $2.56\, c_{DS}$ |

The channel impulse response is then:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-27 -->
$$H_{u,s}^{NLOS}(\tau,t) = \sum_{n=1}^{2}\sum_{i=1}^{3}\sum_{m\in R_i} H_{u,s,n,m}^{NLOS}(t)\,\delta(\tau-\tau_{n,i}) + \sum_{n=3}^{N} H_{u,s,n}^{NLOS}(t)\,\delta(\tau-\tau_n)$$

where $H_{u,s,n}^{NLOS}(t)$ is Eq. 7.5-22 and $H_{u,s,n,m}^{NLOS}(t)$ is its per-ray form (Eq. 7.5-22's summand, evaluated for a single ray $m$ rather than summed over $M$):

<!-- Eq. 7.5-28 -->
$$H_{u,s,n,m}^{NLOS}(t) = \sqrt{\frac{P_n}{M}}\begin{bmatrix} F_{rx,u,\theta}(\theta_{n,m,ZOA},\phi_{n,m,AOA}) \\ F_{rx,u,\phi}(\theta_{n,m,ZOA},\phi_{n,m,AOA}) \end{bmatrix}^T
\begin{bmatrix} \exp(j\Phi_{n,m}^{\theta\theta}) & \sqrt{\kappa_{n,m}^{-1}}\exp(j\Phi_{n,m}^{\theta\phi}) \\ \sqrt{\kappa_{n,m}^{-1}}\exp(j\Phi_{n,m}^{\phi\theta}) & \exp(j\Phi_{n,m}^{\phi\phi}) \end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix} F_{tx,s,\theta}(\theta_{n,m,ZOD},\phi_{n,m,AOD}) \\ F_{tx,s,\phi}(\theta_{n,m,ZOD},\phi_{n,m,AOD}) \end{bmatrix}
\exp\left(j2\pi\frac{\hat r_{rx,n,m}^T \cdot \bar d_{rx,u}}{\lambda_0}\right)
\exp\left(j2\pi\frac{\hat r_{tx,n,m}^T \cdot \bar d_{tx,s}}{\lambda_0}\right)
\exp\left(j2\pi\frac{\hat r_{rx,n,m}^T \cdot \bar v}{\lambda_0}t\right)$$

In the LOS case, a separate LOS channel coefficient is computed:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-29 -->
$$H_{u,s,1}^{LOS}(t) = \begin{bmatrix} F_{rx,u,\theta}(\theta_{LOS,ZOA},\phi_{LOS,AOA}) \\ F_{rx,u,\phi}(\theta_{LOS,ZOA},\phi_{LOS,AOA}) \end{bmatrix}^T
\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix} F_{tx,s,\theta}(\theta_{LOS,ZOD},\phi_{LOS,AOD}) \\ F_{tx,s,\phi}(\theta_{LOS,ZOD},\phi_{LOS,AOD}) \end{bmatrix}
\exp\left(-j2\pi\frac{d_{3D}}{\lambda_0}\right)
\exp\left(j2\pi\frac{\hat r_{rx,LOS}^T \cdot \bar d_{rx,u}}{\lambda_0}\right)
\exp\left(j2\pi\frac{\hat r_{tx,LOS}^T \cdot \bar d_{tx,s}}{\lambda_0}\right)
\exp\left(j2\pi\frac{\hat r_{rx,LOS}^T \cdot \bar v}{\lambda_0}t\right)$$

and combined with the NLOS impulse response, scaled by the desired Ricean K-factor $K_R$:

<!-- Eq. 7.5-30 -->
$$H_{u,s}^{LOS}(\tau,t) = \sqrt{\frac{1}{K_R+1}}H_{u,s}^{NLOS}(\tau,t) + \sqrt{\frac{K_R}{K_R+1}}H_{u,s,1}^{LOS}(t)\,\delta(\tau-\tau_1)$$

## Step 12: apply pathloss and shadowing

Finally, the pathloss (§7.4.1) and shadow fading (§7.4) generated for each BS-UT link in Steps 2-3 are applied to the channel coefficients from Step 11, giving the final channel realization.
