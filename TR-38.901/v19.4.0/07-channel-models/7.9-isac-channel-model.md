# 7.9 Channel model(s) for ISAC

Clause 7.9 defines the **Integrated Sensing and Communication (ISAC)** channel model — a Release-19 addition to TR 38.901. It builds on the communication channel model of the earlier Clause 7 (pathloss, LOS probability, fast fading) and extends it with the known properties (location, Radar Cross-Section, polarization) of one or more **physical objects**. A physical object is either a **sensing target (ST)** — the object of interest — or an **environment object (EO)** with known location. A first type of EO (type-1 EO) shares a ST's characteristics and is modelled the same way; a second type (type-2 EO, §7.9.5.2) is large and modelled differently.

The ISAC channel between a pair of **sensing transmitter (STX)** and **sensing receiver (SRX)** is a common framework composed of one or more **target channel(s)** and a **background channel**. The target channel of ST *k* (for *1 ≤ k ≤ K*, where *K* is the number of STs) contains all multipath components impacted by that ST; the background channel contains the remaining components. The large- and small-scale parameters between any two of STX, ST and SRX are obtained from the reference TR for the corresponding **communication** scenario, unless §7.9 specifies an update — the ST is treated as a receiver (for a STX–ST link) or a transmitter (for a ST–SRX link).

## 7.9.1 Scenarios

A sensing scenario **X** reuses the TRPs and UEs of the corresponding communication scenario X, where X ∈ {UMi, UMa, RMa, InH, InF, UMi-AV, UMa-AV, RMa-AV, Urban grid, Highway, High Speed Train (HST)}. By the type of ST, the sensing scenarios group into five families: **UAV** (ISAC-UAV), **Automotive** (ISAC-Automotive), **Human** (ISAC-Human), **AGV** (ISAC-AGV), and **Objects creating hazards on roads/railways**. The tables below define the evaluation parameters for each; they can be used for channel model calibration.

## 7.9.2 Physical object model

A ST is modelled with one or multiple **scattering points**. Each scattering point of a ST (**SPST**) models the total scattering of some adjacent scattering centres at the ST. A SPST impacts the channel through at least the **RCS** (§7.9.2.1) and the **cross-polarization matrix** (§7.9.2.2).

### 7.9.2.1 RCS of a sensing target

The RCS of a SPST is a scalar defined in the LCS of the ST, dependent on both the incident and scattered angle (same incident/scattered angle → *monostatic* RCS). The RCS-related coefficient $\sigma_{RCS}$ of a SPST for a pair of incident/scattered angles factors as $\sigma_{RCS} = \sigma_M \sigma_D \sigma_S$: a deterministic mean $\sigma_M$ (part of the large-scale parameters, Step 15 in §7.9.4.1); an angular component $\sigma_D$ (fixed to 1, or angular-dependent); and a log-normal $\sigma_S$ (part of the small-scale parameters, Step 10 in §7.9.4.1). The mean $\mu_{\sigma_{S\_dB}}$ and standard deviation $\sigma_{\sigma_{S\_dB}}$ characterizing $10\lg(\sigma_S)$ satisfy a fixed relation:

<!-- Eq. 7.9.2-1 -->
$$\mu_{\sigma_{S\_dB}} = \frac{-\ln(10)}{20}\, \sigma_{\sigma_{S\_dB}}^2$$

**RCS model 1** characterizes a ST as a single SPST with $\sigma_D$ fixed to 1, where $\sigma_M$ is the mean of linear monostatic RCS. For a UAV of small size and a human (RCS model 1), the RCS pattern $10\lg(\sigma_M \sigma_D)$, denoted $\sigma_{\mathrm{MD\_dB}}(\theta_i,\phi_i,\theta_s,\phi_s)$, is deterministic in the incident angle $(\theta_i,\phi_i)$ and scattered angle $(\theta_s,\phi_s)$:

<!-- Eq. 7.9.2-2 -->
$$\sigma_{\mathrm{MD\_dB}}(\theta_i,\phi_i,\theta_s,\phi_s) = \max\left(10\lg(\sigma_M) - 3\sin\!\left(\frac{\beta}{2}\right),\ \sigma_{\mathrm{FS}}(\theta_i,\phi_i,\theta_s,\phi_s)\right)$$

where $\beta \in [0°, 180°]$ is the bistatic angle between the incident and scattering rays within the plane defined by $(\theta_i,\phi_i)$ and $(\theta_s,\phi_s)$; and $\sigma_{\mathrm{FS}}$ is for forward scattering and is set to $-\infty$. The logarithmic values of $\sigma_M$, $\sigma_S$ are:

**RCS model 2** adopts an angular-dependent $\sigma_D$ per SPST and splits a ST into single or multiple SPSTs (single for a UAV of large size and a human; single or multiple for vehicle and AGV — the recommended five points being front, left, back, right and roof). The ST orientation in LCS: the face of a human / the front of a vehicle, a large UAV or an AGV faces azimuth $\phi = 0°$ and zenith $\theta = 90°$ (the AGV front being its short horizontal edge); the top of the ST faces zenith $\theta = 0°$. For these targets, the RCS pattern is:

<!-- Eq. 7.9.2-3 -->
$$\sigma_{\mathrm{MD\_dB}}(\theta_i,\phi_i,\theta_s,\phi_s) = \max\!\Big(G_{max} - \min\{-(\sigma^V_{\mathrm{dB}}(\theta) + \sigma^H_{\mathrm{dB}}(\phi)),\ \sigma_{max}\} - k_1\sin\!\left(\frac{k_2\beta}{2}\right) + 5\log_{10}\!\left(\cos\!\left(\frac{\beta}{2}\right)\right),\ G_{max} - \sigma_{max},\ \sigma_{\mathrm{FS}}(\theta_i,\phi_i,\theta_s,\phi_s)\Big)$$

with $\sigma^V_{\mathrm{dB}}(\theta)$ and $\sigma^H_{\mathrm{dB}}(\phi)$ defined by

$$\sigma^V_{\mathrm{dB}}(\theta) = -\min\!\left\{12\left(\frac{\theta - \theta_{center}}{\theta_{3dB}}\right)^2,\ \sigma_{max}\right\}, \qquad \sigma^H_{\mathrm{dB}}(\phi) = -\min\!\left\{12\left(\frac{\phi - \phi_{center}}{\phi_{3dB}}\right)^2,\ \sigma_{max}\right\}$$

where $(\theta,\phi)$ are the zenith/azimuth of the bisector between the incident ray $(\theta_i,\phi_i)$ and scattered ray $(\theta_s,\phi_s)$; $\beta \in [0°, 180°]$ is the bistatic angle; $\sigma_{\mathrm{FS}}$ is set to $-\infty$; and the shape parameters $(k_1, k_2)$ per target are:

| Target | k₁ | k₂ |
|---|---|---|
| UAV with large size | 6.05 | 1.33 |
| Human with RCS model 2 | 0.5714 | 0.1 |
| Vehicle with single/multiple SPSTs | 6 | 1.65 |
| AGV with single/multiple SPSTs | 12 | 1.45 |

For a ST split into $N_{sp}$ scattering points, each is characterized by one of the $N_{sp}$ sets. The parameters for the different STs (Tables 7.9.2.1-2 … 7.9.2.1-7) follow.

### 7.9.2.2 Cross-polarization matrix of a sensing target

The cross-polarization matrix $CPM_{sp,i}$ of a SPST for a pair *i* of incident/scattered angles is defined in the LCS of the ST and is modelled by amplitude factors $\alpha_{i,1}, \alpha_{i,2}, \beta_{i,1}, \beta_{i,2}$ and initial random phases $\{\Phi^{\theta\theta}_{sp,i}, \Phi^{\theta\phi}_{sp,i}, \Phi^{\phi\theta}_{sp,i}, \Phi^{\phi\phi}_{sp,i}\}$:

<!-- Eq. 7.9.2-4 -->
$$CPM_{sp,i} = \begin{bmatrix} \alpha_{i,1}\exp(j\Phi^{\theta\theta}_{sp,i}) & \beta_{i,1}\exp(j\Phi^{\theta\phi}_{sp,i}) \\ \beta_{i,2}\exp(j\Phi^{\phi\theta}_{sp,i}) & \alpha_{i,2}\exp(j\Phi^{\phi\phi}_{sp,i}) \end{bmatrix}$$

For UAV, human, vehicle and AGV, $\alpha_{i,1} = \alpha_{i,2} = 1$ and $\beta_{i,1} = \beta_{i,2} = \sqrt{\kappa_{sp,i}^{-1}}$, i.e.,

<!-- Eq. 7.9.2-5 -->
$$CPM_{sp,i} = \begin{bmatrix} \exp(j\Phi^{\theta\theta}_{sp,i}) & \sqrt{\kappa_{sp,i}^{-1}}\,\exp(j\Phi^{\theta\phi}_{sp,i}) \\ \sqrt{\kappa_{sp,i}^{-1}}\,\exp(j\Phi^{\phi\theta}_{sp,i}) & \exp(j\Phi^{\phi\phi}_{sp,i}) \end{bmatrix}$$

where $\kappa_{sp,i}$ is the XPR of the pair *i*, randomly generated by a log-normal distribution per target type (Table 7.9.2.2-1); and $\{\Phi^{\theta\theta}_{sp,i}, \Phi^{\theta\phi}_{sp,i}, \Phi^{\phi\theta}_{sp,i}, \Phi^{\phi\phi}_{sp,i}\}$ are uniformly distributed within $(-\pi, \pi)$.

## 7.9.4 Fast fading model

The ISAC channel between a pair of STX and SRX is the sum of the target channel(s) and the background channel. A stepwise procedure (Figure 7.9.4-1) generates the ISAC channel, **referring to the §7.5 procedure** with parameters derived by Tables 7.9.3-2 and 7.9.3-3. Step 1 is executed once, then the per-link steps for the target channel (7.9.4.1) and background channel (7.9.4.2) run, and finally the two are combined (7.9.4.3).

**Step 1 (common):** Set the environment, network layout and antenna-array parameters — choose a sensing scenario and its parameters; give the numbers of STX/ST/SRX and the SPST(s) per ST; give 3D locations of STX/SRX and of each SPST (determining LOS AOD/ZOD/AOA/ZOA); give antenna field patterns $F_{rx}$, $F_{tx}$ and array geometries; give array orientations; give the speed and direction of motion of STX, ST and SRX; and specify the centre frequency and bandwidth. For monostatic sensing, a STX and its corresponding SRX are co-located and share antenna pattern, orientation, speed and direction.

### 7.9.4.1 Target channel

Following Step 1, the target channel for one or multiple STs between a STX/SRX pair is generated per the following steps (each reusing the corresponding §7.5 step for both the **STX–SPST** link and the **SPST–SRX** link, with the SPST–SRX link using primed cluster/ray indices $n', m'$).

**Large-scale parameters — Steps 2–4:** assign LOS/NLOS per STX–SPST and SPST–SRX pair (Step 2, using the LOS-probability equations of the reference TRs in §7.9.3); calculate pathloss (Step 3); and generate the large-scale parameters — DS, angular spreads (ASA, ASD, ZSA, ZSD), Ricean K-factor, shadow fading — with the applicability limits of §7.5 (Step 4; ASA/ASD limited to 104°, ZSA/ZSD to 52°). For monostatic sensing, the large-scale parameters are identical for a STX–SPST link and its corresponding SPST–SRX link.

**Small-scale parameters — Steps 5–12:** generate cluster delays (Step 5), cluster powers (Step 6), arrival/departure angles (Step 7), ray coupling within a cluster (Step 8) — each per the corresponding §7.5 step for both links. **Step 9** couples the rays of a STX–SPST link with those of the corresponding SPST–SRX link: the LOS ray in one link couples with the LOS ray in the other (never dropped), and NLOS rays couple per one of two options (Option 1: each-with-each; Option 2: 1-by-1 random coupling). The set of coupled rays is $R_0$.

**Step 10** obtains the power of each path $(k,p,n',m',n,m)$:

<!-- Eq. 7.9.4-1 -->
$$P_{n',m',n,m}^{k,p} = \sigma_{D,n',m',n,m}^{k,p}\, \min\!\left(\sigma_{S,n',m',n,m}^{k,p},\ 10^{(\mu_{\sigma_{S\_dB}} + 3\sigma_{\sigma_{S\_dB}})/10}\right) P_{rx,n',m'}^{k,p}\, P_{tx,n,m}^{k,p}$$

where $\sigma_{D}$, $\sigma_{S}$ are the second and third RCS components (derived from the incident AOA/ZOA in the STX–SPST link and the scattered AOD/ZOD in the SPST–SRX link, per the RCS model of §7.9.2.1). The per-link ray powers $P_{tx,n,m}^{k,p}$, $P_{rx,n',m'}^{k,p}$ follow the §7.5 Ricean split — for a LOS STX–SPST link, $P_{tx,0,0}^{k,p} = \tfrac{K_{tx,R}^{k,p}}{K_{tx,R}^{k,p}+1}$ for the LOS ray and $P_{tx,n,m}^{k,p} = \tfrac{1}{K_{tx,R}^{k,p}+1}\tfrac{P_{tx,n}^{k,p}}{M}$ for NLOS rays; for a NLOS link, $P_{tx,n,m}^{k,p} = \tfrac{P_{tx,n}^{k,p}}{M}$ (analogously for the SPST–SRX link). A path with power more than $X$ dB below the maximum ($X$ up to $-25$/$-40$ dB for coupling Options 1/2) is dropped; the remaining set is $R$.

**Step 11** obtains the absolute delay of each path:

<!-- Eq. 7.9.4-2 -->
$$\tau_{n',m',n,m}^{k,p} = \tau_{rx,n',m'}^{k,p} + d_{rx,3D}^{k,p}/c + \Delta\tau_{rx}^{k,p} + \tau_{tx,n,m}^{k,p} + d_{tx,3D}^{k,p}/c + \Delta\tau_{tx}^{k,p}$$

where $d_{tx,3D}^{k,p}$, $d_{rx,3D}^{k,p}$ are the STX–SPST and SPST–SRX 3D distances, and $\Delta\tau_{rx}^{k,p}$, $\Delta\tau_{tx}^{k,p}$ are absolute-delay offsets per Table 7.6.9-1. For monostatic sensing, $\Delta\tau_{rx}^{k,p} = \Delta\tau_{tx}^{k,p}$.

**Step 12** generates the cross-polarization power ratios (XPR) $\kappa$ for each path, log-normal distributed:

<!-- Eq. 7.9.4-3 -->
$$\kappa_{n',m',m,n}^{k,p} = 10^{X_{n',m',m,n}^{k,p}/10}, \qquad X_{n',m',m,n}^{k,p} \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu_{XPR}, \sigma_{XPR}^2)$$

with $\mu_{XPR}$, $\sigma_{XPR}$ from Table 7.9.2.2-1.

**Coefficient generation — Steps 13–15. Step 13** draws initial random phases $\{\Phi^{\theta\theta}, \Phi^{\theta\phi}, \Phi^{\phi\theta}, \Phi^{\phi\phi}\}$ (uniform in $(-\pi,\pi)$) for each path. **Step 14** generates the channel coefficient of each path for each Rx/Tx element pair $u,s$:

<!-- Eq. 7.9.4-4 -->
$$H_{u,s,n',m',n,m}^{k,p}(t) = \sqrt{P_{n',m',n,m}^{k,p}}\, \begin{bmatrix} F_{rx,u,\theta}(\theta_{rx,n',m',ZOA}^{k,p}, \phi_{rx,n',m',AOA}^{k,p}) \\ F_{rx,u,\phi}(\theta_{rx,n',m',ZOA}^{k,p}, \phi_{rx,n',m',AOA}^{k,p}) \end{bmatrix}^T \frac{CPM_{rx,n',m'}^{k,p}\, CPM_{n',m',n,m}^{k,p}\, CPM_{tx,n,m}^{k,p}}{\sqrt{(|d^{\theta\theta}|^2 + |d^{\phi\phi}|^2)/2}} \begin{bmatrix} F_{tx,s,\theta}(\theta_{tx,n,m,ZOD}^{k,p}, \phi_{tx,n,m,AOD}^{k,p}) \\ F_{tx,s,\phi}(\theta_{tx,n,m,ZOD}^{k,p}, \phi_{tx,n,m,AOD}^{k,p}) \end{bmatrix} \exp\!\left(j2\pi\frac{\hat{r}_{rx,k,p,n',m'}^T(t)\cdot\bar{d}_{rx,u} + \hat{r}_{tx,k,p,n,m}^T(t)\cdot\bar{d}_{tx,s}}{\lambda_0}\right) \exp\!\left(j2\pi\int_{t_0}^{t} f_{D,n',m',n,m}^{k,p}(\tilde{t})\,d\tilde{t}\right)$$

with the Doppler frequency

<!-- Eq. 7.9.4-5 -->
$$f_{D,n',m',n,m}^{k,p}(t) = \frac{\hat{r}_{rx,k,p,n',m'}^T(t)\,\bar{v}_{rx}(t) + \hat{r}_{k,p,n',m'}^T(t)\,\bar{v}_{k,p}(t)}{\lambda_0} + \frac{\hat{r}_{tx,k,p,n,m}^T(t)\,\bar{v}_{tx}(t) + \hat{r}_{k,p,n,m}^T(t)\,\bar{v}_{k,p}(t)}{\lambda_0}$$

where $CPM_{n',m',n,m}^{k,p}$ is the polarization matrix of the SPST $p$ (and $CPM_{n,m,n',m'}^{k,p} = [CPM_{n',m',n,m}^{k,p}]^T$ for monostatic sensing):

<!-- Eq. 7.9.4-6 -->
$$CPM_{n',m',n,m}^{k,p} = \begin{bmatrix} \exp(j\Phi_{n',m',m,n}^{k,p,\theta\theta}) & \sqrt{\kappa_{n',m',n,m}^{k,p}{}^{-1}}\exp(j\Phi_{n',m',m,n}^{k,p,\theta\phi}) \\ \sqrt{\kappa_{n',m',n,m}^{k,p}{}^{-1}}\exp(j\Phi_{n',m',m,n}^{k,p,\phi\theta}) & \exp(j\Phi_{n',m',m,n}^{k,p,\phi\phi}) \end{bmatrix}$$

$CPM_{tx,n,m}^{k,p}$ is the polarization matrix of the ray in the STX–SPST link — $\begin{bmatrix}1&0\\0&-1\end{bmatrix}$ for the LOS ray, and for a NLOS ray:

<!-- Eq. 7.9.4-7 -->
$$CPM_{tx,n,m}^{k,p} = \begin{bmatrix} \exp(j\Phi_{tx,n,m}^{k,p,\theta\theta}) & \sqrt{\kappa_{tx,n,m}^{k,p}{}^{-1}}\exp(j\Phi_{tx,n,m}^{k,p,\theta\phi}) \\ \sqrt{\kappa_{tx,n,m}^{k,p}{}^{-1}}\exp(j\Phi_{tx,n,m}^{k,p,\phi\theta}) & \exp(j\Phi_{tx,n,m}^{k,p,\phi\phi}) \end{bmatrix}$$

$CPM_{rx,n',m'}^{k,p}$ is the polarization matrix of the ray in the SPST–SRX link — $\begin{bmatrix}1&0\\0&-1\end{bmatrix}$ for the LOS ray, and for a NLOS ray:

<!-- Eq. 7.9.4-8 -->
$$CPM_{rx,n',m'}^{k,p} = \begin{bmatrix} \exp(j\Phi_{rx,n',m'}^{k,p,\theta\theta}) & \sqrt{\kappa_{rx,n',m'}^{k,p}{}^{-1}}\exp(j\Phi_{rx,n',m'}^{k,p,\theta\phi}) \\ \sqrt{\kappa_{rx,n',m'}^{k,p}{}^{-1}}\exp(j\Phi_{rx,n',m'}^{k,p,\phi\theta}) & \exp(j\Phi_{rx,n',m'}^{k,p,\phi\phi}) \end{bmatrix}$$

$d^{\theta\theta}, d^{\phi\phi}$ are the main-diagonal elements of $CPM_{rx,n',m'}^{k,p}\, CPM_{n',m',n,m}^{k,p}\, CPM_{tx,n,m}^{k,p}$. The spherical unit vectors are:

<!-- Eq. 7.9.4-9 -->
$$\hat{r}_{rx,k,p,n',m'} = \begin{bmatrix} \sin\theta_{rx,n',m',ZOA}^{k,p}\cos\phi_{rx,n',m',AOA}^{k,p} \\ \sin\theta_{rx,n',m',ZOA}^{k,p}\sin\phi_{rx,n',m',AOA}^{k,p} \\ \cos\theta_{rx,n',m',ZOA}^{k,p} \end{bmatrix} \quad\text{(SRX → SPST)}$$

<!-- Eq. 7.9.4-10 -->
$$\hat{r}_{tx,k,p,n,m} = \begin{bmatrix} \sin\theta_{tx,n,m,ZOD}^{k,p}\cos\phi_{tx,n,m,AOD}^{k,p} \\ \sin\theta_{tx,n,m,ZOD}^{k,p}\sin\phi_{tx,n,m,AOD}^{k,p} \\ \cos\theta_{tx,n,m,ZOD}^{k,p} \end{bmatrix} \quad\text{(STX → SPST)}$$

<!-- Eq. 7.9.4-11 -->
$$\hat{r}_{k,p,n',m'} = \begin{bmatrix} \sin\theta_{rx,n',m',ZOD}^{k,p}\cos\phi_{rx,n',m',AOD}^{k,p} \\ \sin\theta_{rx,n',m',ZOD}^{k,p}\sin\phi_{rx,n',m',AOD}^{k,p} \\ \cos\theta_{rx,n',m',ZOD}^{k,p} \end{bmatrix} \quad\text{(SPST → SRX)}$$

<!-- Eq. 7.9.4-12 -->
$$\hat{r}_{k,p,n,m} = \begin{bmatrix} \sin\theta_{tx,n,m,ZOA}^{k,p}\cos\phi_{tx,n,m,AOA}^{k,p} \\ \sin\theta_{tx,n,m,ZOA}^{k,p}\sin\phi_{tx,n,m,AOA}^{k,p} \\ \cos\theta_{tx,n,m,ZOA}^{k,p} \end{bmatrix} \quad\text{(SPST → STX)}$$

with $\bar{v}_{rx}, \bar{v}_{tx}$ the velocities of SRX, STX and $\bar{v}_{k,p} = \bar{v}_{ma,k} + \bar{v}_{mi,k,p}$ the velocity of SPST $p$ of ST $k$ (ST velocity plus micro-motion). The channel impulse response of SPST $p$ of ST $k$ is

<!-- Eq. 7.9.4-13 -->
$$H_{u,s}^{k,p}(\tau,t) = \sum_{(n',m',n,m)\in R} H_{u,s,n',m',n,m}^{k,p}(t)\, \delta\!\left(\tau - \tau_{n',m',n,m}^{k,p}\right)$$

**Step 15** applies pathloss, shadowing and the first RCS component $\sigma_M$, summing over the $P$ SPSTs to form the target channel for ST $k$:

<!-- Eq. 7.9.4-14 -->
$$H_{u,s}^{(k)}(\tau,t) = \sum_{p} \left(10^{-(PL_{tx,k,p} + PL_{rx,k,p} + SF_{tx,k,p} + SF_{rx,k,p})/20} \cdot \sqrt{\frac{4\pi\sigma_M^{k,p}}{\lambda_0^2}} \cdot H_{u,s}^{(k,p)}(\tau,t)\right)$$

where $PL_{tx,k,p}$, $PL_{rx,k,p}$ are the STX–SPST and SPST–SRX pathlosses, $SF_{tx,k,p}$, $SF_{rx,k,p}$ their shadow fadings, and $\sigma_M^{k,p}$ the first RCS component (§7.9.2.1).

### 7.9.4.2 Background channel

For **TRP-monostatic and UE-monostatic** sensing, the background channel is generated via a reference-point procedure: **Step 2** draws 3 reference points (RPs) whose 2D distance and height are drawn as $G(\alpha_d, \beta_d) + c_d$ and $G(\alpha_h, \beta_h) + c_h$ (with parameters in Tables 7.9.4.2-1/2), where $G(\alpha, \beta)$ is the shape-rate Gamma distribution $f(x|\alpha,\beta) = \frac{\beta^\alpha}{\Gamma(\alpha)}x^{\alpha-1}e^{-\beta x}$, and the LOS AODs to the 3 RPs are rotated by $\tfrac{2}{3}\pi$ and $\tfrac{4}{3}\pi$. **Steps 3–4** assign NLOS and generate each STX/SRX–RP channel per §7.5 Steps 3–12 and §7.6.10. **Step 5** sums the 3 channels after applying pathloss and shadowing:

### 7.9.4.3 Combining target channel and background channel

## 7.9.5 Additional modelling components

Similar to §7.6, these components support advanced simulations (spatial consistency, known type-2 EOs, target/background power adjustment, moving scatterers, lower-power clusters, blockage). They are computationally more expensive and may not be required in all evaluation cases.

### 7.9.5.1 Spatial consistency

The spatial-consistency procedure generates spatially-consistent random variables for the STX–SPST links, the SPST–SRX links and the background channels. For UMi-AV/UMa-AV/RMa-AV, the 2D horizontal random process can be extended to 3D. The §7.6.3 procedures are reused for TRP↔ST/UT links (an RSU-type UE is treated as a TRP), with new UT-UT/SPST links classified as **Link-correlated** (parameters of any two links are correlated subject to correlation distance) or **All-correlated** (all links correlated). Spatial consistency is **not** modelled across links of different types/floors, between the background channel and any link for monostatic (or between any links for bistatic) sensing, across non-co-located TRPs, or between TRP-TRP and other links (see the source TR for the full exclusion list). The spatial consistency across links between STX/SRX and multiple SPSTs of one ST is modelled as if the SPSTs were multiple STs.

### 7.9.5.2 Type-2 environment object

A type-2 EO (e.g. a wall) is a finite-size surface; a ray specularly reflected by it can be modelled in the STX–SPST link, SPST–SRX link and optionally the background channel, if a specular reflection point lies within the surface. The general procedure (incident ray from Tx, scattered ray to Rx):

**Step A — check whether a reflected ray needs modelling.** The type-2 EO is a plane perpendicular to the ground:

<!-- Eq. 7.9.5-1 -->
$$Ax + By = D, \quad x\in[x_{left}, x_{right}],\ y\in[y_{left}, y_{right}],\ z\in[z_{left}, z_{right}]$$

with $[A, B, 0]$ the normal vector (pointing along the EO orientation). The reflection point $[x_w, y_w, z_w]$ is

<!-- Eq. 7.9.5-2 -->
$$x_w = x_{tx'} + \frac{(x_{rx}-x_{tx'})(D - Ax_{tx'} - By_{tx'})}{A(x_{rx}-x_{tx'}) + B(y_{rx}-y_{tx'})}$$

<!-- Eq. 7.9.5-3 -->
$$y_w = y_{tx'} + \frac{(y_{rx}-y_{tx'})(D - Ax_{tx'} - By_{tx'})}{A(x_{rx}-x_{tx'}) + B(y_{rx}-y_{tx'})}$$

<!-- Eq. 7.9.5-4 -->
$$z_w = z_{tx'} + \frac{(z_{rx}-z_{tx'})(D - Ax_{tx'} - By_{tx'})}{A(x_{rx}-x_{tx'}) + B(y_{rx}-y_{tx'})}$$

where $[x_{tx'}, y_{tx'}, z_{tx'}]$ is the reflection image of Tx: $x_{tx'} = x_{tx} - \tfrac{2A(Ax_{tx}+By_{tx}-D)}{A^2+B^2}$, $y_{tx'} = y_{tx} - \tfrac{2B(Ax_{tx}+By_{tx}-D)}{A^2+B^2}$, $z_{tx'} = z_{tx}$. If $x_w, y_w, z_w$ lie within the EO bounds, the reflection point is on its surface; otherwise there is no reflection ray.

**Step B — generate the reflected-ray parameters.** The 3D distance Tx→reflection point→Rx:

<!-- Eq. 7.9.5-5 -->
$$d_{EO} = \sqrt{(x_{tx'}-x_{rx})^2 + (y_{tx'}-y_{rx})^2 + (z_{tx'}-z_{rx})^2}$$

The ZoD/AoD from Tx and ZoA/AoA from Rx to the reflection point:

<!-- Eq. 7.9.5-6 -->
$$\theta_{EO,ZOD} = \arccos\!\left(\frac{z_w - z_{tx}}{\sqrt{(x_w-x_{tx})^2+(y_w-y_{tx})^2+(z_w-z_{tx})^2}}\right)$$

<!-- Eq. 7.9.5-7 -->
$$\phi_{EO,AOD} = \operatorname{atan2}(y_w - y_{tx},\ x_w - x_{tx})$$

<!-- Eq. 7.9.5-8 -->
$$\theta_{EO,ZOA} = \arccos\!\left(\frac{z_w - z_{rx}}{\sqrt{(x_w-x_{rx})^2+(y_w-y_{rx})^2+(z_w-z_{rx})^2}}\right)$$

<!-- Eq. 7.9.5-9 -->
$$\phi_{EO,AOA} = \operatorname{atan2}(y_w - y_{rx},\ x_w - x_{rx})$$

The effective polarization matrix of the type-2 EO reflection ray:

<!-- Eq. 7.9.5-10 -->
$$CPM_{EO} = \begin{bmatrix} \cos\gamma_2 & -\sin\gamma_2 \\ \sin\gamma_2 & \cos\gamma_2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} R_{\parallel} & 0 \\ 0 & -R_{\perp} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \cos\gamma_1 & \sin\gamma_1 \\ -\sin\gamma_1 & \cos\gamma_1 \end{bmatrix}$$

where $\gamma_1 = \operatorname{atan2}(-e_{\theta_{EO,ZOD}}\, n_{plane},\ e_{\phi_{EO,AOD}}\, n_{plane})$ and $\gamma_2 = \operatorname{atan2}(e_{\theta_{EO,ZOA}}\, n_{plane},\ -e_{\phi_{EO,AOA}}\, n_{plane})$, with $n_{plane}$ the incident-plane normal and $e_{\theta}, e_{\phi}$ the spherical basis vectors of the incident/scattered rays. The reflection coefficients $R_{\parallel}$, $R_{\perp}$ reuse formulas 7.6-38/7.6-39 of §7.6.8 with the modified incidence angle

<!-- Eq. 7.9.5-11 -->
$$\theta_{EO} = \arccos\!\left(\frac{(x_w-x_{tx})A + (y_w-y_{tx})B}{\sqrt{A^2+B^2}\,\sqrt{(x_w-x_{tx})^2+(y_w-y_{tx})^2+(z_w-z_{tx})^2}}\right)$$

<!-- Eq. 7.9.5-12 -->
$$R_{\parallel} = \frac{\frac{\varepsilon_{EO}}{\varepsilon_0}\cos(\theta_{EO}) + \sqrt{\frac{\varepsilon_{EO}}{\varepsilon_0} - \sin^2(\theta_{EO})}}{\frac{\varepsilon_{EO}}{\varepsilon_0}\cos(\theta_{EO}) - \sqrt{\frac{\varepsilon_{EO}}{\varepsilon_0} - \sin^2(\theta_{EO})}}$$

<!-- Eq. 7.9.5-13 -->
$$R_{\perp} = \frac{\cos(\theta_{EO}) + \sqrt{\frac{\varepsilon_{EO}}{\varepsilon_0} - \sin^2(\theta_{EO})}}{\cos(\theta_{EO}) - \sqrt{\frac{\varepsilon_{EO}}{\varepsilon_0} - \sin^2(\theta_{EO})}}$$

When a type-2 EO is deployed, the ISAC channel generation of §7.9.4/7.9.4.1/7.9.4.2 is modified: Step 1 additionally gives the number, 3D locations and orientation of each type-2 EO; Step 2 (7.9.4.1) offers two options for the LOS condition (Option 1 sets LOS probability to 0 if the EO blocks the LOS ray; Option 2 leaves Step 2 unchanged); and a step inserted between Steps 8 and 9 generates the NLOS rays specularly reflected by the EO (see the source TR for the full modification list).

### 7.9.5.3 Power normalization across target channel and background channel

To combine the target and background channels, power normalization can be applied to keep the same/similar channel power as the background channel without a sensing target.

### 7.9.5.4 Doppler of mobile scatterers

To support scatterer mobility, the Doppler frequency $f_{D,n',m',n,m}^{k,p}(t)$ in Step 14 (§7.9.4.1) is updated with extra scatterer-motion terms:

<!-- Eq. 7.9.5-14 -->
$$f_{D,n',m',n,m}^{k,p}(t) = \frac{\hat{r}_{rx,k,p,n',m'}^T(t)\bar{v}_{rx}(t) + \hat{r}_{k,p,n',m'}^T(t)\bar{v}_{k,p}(t) + 2\alpha_{rx,n',m'}^{k,p} D_{rx,n',m'}^{k,p}}{\lambda_0} + \frac{\hat{r}_{tx,k,p,n,m}^T(t)\bar{v}_{tx}(t) + \hat{r}_{k,p,n,m}^T(t)\bar{v}_{k,p}(t) + 2\alpha_{tx,n,m}^{k,p} D_{tx,n,m}^{k,p}}{\lambda_0}$$

where $D_{rx,n',m'}^{k,p}$, $D_{tx,n,m}^{k,p}$ are uniform in $[-v_{scatt}, v_{scatt}]$ ($v_{scatt}$ = maximum clutter speed), and $\alpha_{rx,n',m'}^{k,p}$, $\alpha_{tx,n,m}^{k,p}$ are Bernoulli ($p'$/$p$ if $n'>0$/$n>0$, else 0), with the mean $p$ setting the proportion of mobile scatterers.

### 7.9.5.5 Lower power clusters

Additional NLOS clusters/rays can be added to the background channel to simulate weak rays. **Step 1** generates a first background channel per §7.9.4.2. **Step 2** generates a second background channel per §7.9.4.2, reusing the same DS/ASA/ASD/ZSA/ZSD and $C_\theta$/$C_\phi$, with revised parameters: cluster count $N$ increased to 360, rays per cluster $M$ reduced to 1, and NLOS conditions. **Step 3** scales the second channel's cluster/ray power down by $P_{max}^{(S1)}\, 10^{G/10}$ ($G = -25$ dB, $P_{max}^{(S1)}$ = maximum NLOS-cluster power of the first channel) and adds it to the first. The final background channel is 7.9.5-15a (bistatic) / 7.9.5-15b (monostatic):

<!-- Eq. 7.9.5-15a -->
$$H_{u,s}^{bk}(\tau,t) = H_{u,s}^{bk1}(\tau,t) + P_{max}^{(S1)}\, 10^{G/10}\, H_{u,s}^{bk2}(\tau,t) \quad\text{(bistatic)}$$

<!-- Eq. 7.9.5-15b -->
$$H_{u,s}^{bk,r}(\tau,t) = H_{u,s}^{bk1,r}(\tau,t) + P_{max}^{(S1,r)}\, 10^{G/10}\, H_{u,s}^{bk2,r}(\tau,t) \quad\text{(monostatic, reference point } r\text{)}$$
