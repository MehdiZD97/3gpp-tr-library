# 7.4 Pathloss, LOS probability and penetration modelling

This clause covers four related pieces of the large-scale channel model: the pathloss formulas used per deployment scenario (§7.4.1), the probability that a link is line-of-sight (§7.4.2), outdoor-to-indoor (O2I) penetration loss through building walls and car bodies (§7.4.3), and how shadow fading correlates over distance (§7.4.4).

## 7.4.1 Pathloss

Table 7.4.1-1 collects the LOS and NLOS pathloss formulas for every scenario covered by the model (RMa, UMa, UMi-Street Canyon, InH-Office, the four InF sub-scenarios, and SMa), along with the shadow-fading standard deviation and the default antenna-height/distance ranges each formula assumes. Distances are 3D unless a formula explicitly calls out $d_{2D}$. The 3D/2D distance definitions referenced throughout the table are given by:

<!-- Eq. 7.4-1 -->
$$d_{3D-out} + d_{3D-in} = \sqrt{(d_{2D-out}+d_{2D-in})^2 + (h_{BS}-h_{UT})^2}$$

for indoor UTs, where the outdoor/indoor split of the distance and height terms follows the same $d_{2D}$/$d_{3D}$ convention used for outdoor UTs (see Figures 7.4.1-1 and 7.4.1-2 in the source TR).

_A matching CSV lives at `tables/table-7.4.1-1.csv`; the machine-readable YAML breakdown is in `7.4-pathloss.yaml` under the `pathloss` key._

Several scenarios also depend on an *effective* breakpoint distance $d'_{BP}$ or breakpoint distance $d_{BP}$ that folds in an effective antenna height correction — see the TR's own NOTE 1 and NOTE 5 under Table 7.4.1-1 for the exact definitions; they aren't reproduced in full here since they're shared machinery rather than per-scenario content.

## 7.4.2 LOS probability

Table 7.4.2-1 gives the probability that a given link is line-of-sight, as a function of the (2D, outdoor- or indoor-projected) distance between UT and BS. Most scenarios use a simple exponential-decay form beyond a near-field distance where LOS is guaranteed; UMa and the InF/SMa scenarios add extra correction terms (clutter density, UT height, or a mix of commercial/residential/vegetation obstruction probabilities for SMa).

_A matching CSV lives at `tables/table-7.4.2-1.csv`; the machine-readable YAML breakdown is in `7.4-pathloss.yaml` under the `los_probability` key._

| Scenario | LOS probability formula | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| RMa | $Pr_{LOS} = \begin{cases} 1 & d_{2D-out} \le 10m \\ \exp\left(-\dfrac{d_{2D-out}-10}{1000}\right) & 10m < d_{2D-out} \end{cases}$ | — |
| UMi-StreetCanyon | $Pr_{LOS} = \begin{cases} 1 & d_{2D-out} \le 18m \\ \dfrac{18}{d_{2D-out}} + \exp\left(-\dfrac{d_{2D-out}}{36}\right)\left(1-\dfrac{18}{d_{2D-out}}\right) & 18m < d_{2D-out} \end{cases}$ | — |
| UMa | $Pr_{LOS} = \begin{cases} 1 & d_{2D-out} \le 18m \\ \left[\dfrac{18}{d_{2D-out}} + \exp\left(-\dfrac{d_{2D-out}}{63}\right)\left(1-\dfrac{18}{d_{2D-out}}\right)\right]\left(1 + C'(h_{UT})\dfrac{5}{4}\left(\dfrac{d_{2D-out}}{100}\right)^3 \exp\left(-\dfrac{d_{2D-out}}{150}\right)\right) & 18m < d_{2D-out} \end{cases}; \quad C'(h_{UT}) = \begin{cases} 0 & h_{UT} \le 13m \\ \left(\dfrac{h_{UT}-13}{10}\right)^{1.5} & 13m < h_{UT} \le 23m \end{cases}$ | — |
| Indoor-MixedOffice | $Pr_{LOS} = \begin{cases} 1 & d_{2D-in} \le 1.2m \\ \exp\left(-\dfrac{d_{2D-in}-1.2}{4.7}\right) & 1.2m < d_{2D-in} < 6.5m \\ \exp\left(-\dfrac{d_{2D-in}-6.5}{32.6}\right)\cdot 0.32 & 6.5m \le d_{2D-in} \end{cases}$ | — |
| Indoor-OpenOffice | $Pr_{LOS} = \begin{cases} 1 & d_{2D-in} \le 5m \\ \exp\left(-\dfrac{d_{2D-in}-5}{70.8}\right) & 5m < d_{2D-in} \le 49m \\ \exp\left(-\dfrac{d_{2D-in}-49}{211.7}\right)\cdot 0.54 & 49m < d_{2D-in} \end{cases}$ | — |
| InF-SL, InF-DL, InF-SH, InF-DH | $Pr_{LOS,subsce}(d_{2D}) = \exp\left(-\dfrac{d_{2D}}{k_{subsce}}\right); \quad k_{subsce} = \begin{cases} -\dfrac{d_{clutter}}{\ln(1-r)} & \text{for InF-SL and InF-DL} \\ -\dfrac{d_{clutter}}{\ln(1-r)}\cdot\dfrac{h_{BS}-h_{UT}}{h_c-h_{UT}} & \text{for InF-SH and InF-DH} \end{cases}$ | Parameters d_clutter, r, h_c defined in Table 7.2-4 |
| InF-HH | $Pr_{LOS} = 1$ | — |
| SMa | $P_{LOS} = \begin{cases} 1 & d_{2D-out} \le 10m \\ \exp\left(-\dfrac{d_{2D-out}}{k_{commercial}}\right)\exp\left(-\dfrac{d_{2D-out}}{k_{residential}}\right)\exp\left(-\dfrac{d_{2D-out}}{k_{vegetation}}\right) & d_{2D-out} > 10m \end{cases}; \quad \dfrac{1}{k_{commercial}} = -\dfrac{\ln(1-r_{commercial})}{d_{clutter}}\cdot\dfrac{h_{commercial}-h_{UT}}{h_{BS}-h_{UT}}; \quad \dfrac{1}{k_{residential}} = -\dfrac{\ln(1-r_{residential})}{d_{clutter}}\cdot\dfrac{\max(h_{residential}-h_{UT},0)}{h_{BS}-h_{UT}}; \quad \dfrac{1}{k_{vegetation}} = -\dfrac{\ln(1-r_{vegetation})}{d_{clutter}}\cdot\dfrac{h_{vegetation}-h_{UT}}{h_{BS}-h_{UT}}; \quad d_{clutter}=30m, h_{commercial}=20m, h_{residential}=8m, h_{vegetation}=15m, r_{commercial}=2\%, r_{residential}=18\%, r_{vegetation}=0\%/10\%/20\%$ | r_vegetation: 0% no vegetation, 10% sparse vegetation, 20% dense vegetation |

The LOS probabilities above assume default antenna heights (3 m indoor, 10 m for UMi, 25 m for UMa). The InF sub-scenarios share one formula shape but split into two cases depending on whether the sub-scenario has sparse (SL/DL) or dense (SH/DH) clutter; the clutter parameters ($d_{clutter}$, $r$, $h_c$) are defined in Table 7.2-4, outside this clause.

## 7.4.3 O2I penetration loss

### 7.4.3.1 O2I building penetration loss

The pathloss for a UT located indoors, reached from an outdoor BS, adds a building-penetration term on top of the outdoor pathloss from §7.4.1 (with $d_{3D}$ replaced by the outdoor+indoor split $d_{3D-out}+d_{3D-in}$):

<!-- Eq. 7.4-2 -->
$$PL = PL_b + PL_{tw} + PL_{in} + N(0, \sigma_P^2)$$

where $PL_b$ is the outdoor pathloss, $PL_{tw}$ is the loss through the external wall, $PL_{in}$ is the additional loss from depth into the building, and $\sigma_P$ is the penetration-loss standard deviation. The external-wall loss itself is a power-weighted combination of one or more material losses:

<!-- Eq. 7.4-3 -->
$$PL_{tw} = PL_{npi} - 10\log_{10}\sum_{i=1}^{N} \left(p_i \times 10^{-L_{material_i}/10}\right)$$

where $PL_{npi}$ is an additional non-perpendicular-incidence loss, $p_i$ is the fraction of material $i$ in the wall ($\sum p_i = 1$), and $L_{material_i}$ is that material's penetration loss, given in Table 7.4.3-1:

_A matching CSV lives at `tables/table-7.4.3-1.csv`; the machine-readable YAML breakdown is in `7.4-pathloss.yaml` under `o2i_penetration_loss.materials`._

| Material | Penetration loss formula [dB] | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Standard multi-pane glass | $L_{glass} = 2 + 0.2f$ | NOTE 1 |
| IRR glass | $L_{IRRglass} = 25.4 + 0.11f$ | NOTE 1; NOTE 2 |
| Concrete | $L_{concrete} = 5 + 4f$ | NOTE 1 |
| Plywood | $L_{plywood} = 1.03 + 0.17f$ | NOTE 1 |
| Wood | $L_{wood} = 4.85 + 0.12f$ | NOTE 1 |

Table 7.4.3-2 then defines three complete O2I building-penetration models — low-loss, high-loss, and low-loss A — as specific material mixes, each with its own indoor-loss term and standard deviation:

_A matching CSV lives at `tables/table-7.4.3-2.csv`; the machine-readable YAML breakdown is in `7.4-pathloss.yaml` under `o2i_penetration_loss.building_models`._

| Model | Path loss through external wall $PL_{tw}$ [dB] | Indoor loss $PL_{in}$ [dB] | $\sigma_P$ [dB] |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low-loss model | $5 - 10\log_{10}\left(0.3\cdot10^{-L_{glass}/10} + 0.7\cdot10^{-L_{concrete}/10}\right)$ | $0.5\, d_{2D-in}$ | 4.4 |
| High-loss model | $5 - 10\log_{10}\left(0.7\cdot10^{-L_{IRRglass}/10} + 0.3\cdot10^{-L_{concrete}/10}\right)$ | $0.5\, d_{2D-in}$ | 6.5 |
| Low-loss A model | $5 - 10\log_{10}\left(0.3\cdot10^{-L_{glass}/10} + 0.7\cdot10^{-L_{plywood}/10}\right)$ | $0.5\, d_{2D-in}$ | 4.4 |

Applicability differs by scenario: UMa and UMi-Street Canyon support both the low-loss and high-loss models; RMa uses only the low-loss model; InF uses only the high-loss model; SMa supports low-loss, high-loss, and low-loss A. Which mix of low- vs. high-loss buildings to simulate is left to the user, since it depends on regional building practices that the TR does not mandate.

For backwards compatibility with TR 36.873, single-frequency UMa/UMi simulations below 6 GHz may instead use a fixed, non-material-based penetration model (Table 7.4.3-3), which also overrides the shadow-fading std from Table 7.4.1-1 with a fixed value:

_A matching CSV lives at `tables/table-7.4.3-3.csv`; the machine-readable YAML breakdown is in `7.4-pathloss.yaml` under `o2i_penetration_loss.building_single_frequency_below_6ghz`._

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| $PL_{tw}$ | 20 dB |
| $PL_{in}$ | $0.5\, d_{2D-in} \text{, with } d_{2D-in} \text{ a single, link-specific, uniform variable between 0 and 25 m}$ |
| $\sigma_P$ | 0 dB |
| $\sigma_{SF}$ | 7 dB (sigma_SF replaces the respective value in Table 7.4.1-1) |

### 7.4.3.2 O2I car penetration loss

Car penetration loss is modelled as a Gaussian offset on top of the outdoor pathloss rather than a material breakdown:

<!-- Eq. 7.4-4 -->
$$PL = PL_b + N(\mu, \sigma_P^2)$$

with $\mu = 9$ dB and $\sigma_P = 5$ dB by default (optionally $\mu = 20$ dB for metallized car windows), generated independently per UT. This model is applicable from roughly 0.6 to 60 GHz. See `7.4-pathloss.yaml` under `o2i_penetration_loss.car_penetration_loss` for the machine-readable form.

## 7.4.4 Autocorrelation of shadow fading

Shadow fading is modelled as log-normal around the mean pathloss, and adjacent shadow-fading values are spatially correlated. That correlation follows a simple exponential decay with horizontal-plane distance $\Delta x$:

<!-- Eq. 7.4-5 -->
$$R(\Delta x) = e^{-|\Delta x| / d_{cor}}$$

where the correlation length $d_{cor}$ is scenario-dependent and is defined alongside the other large-scale-parameter correlation distances in Table 7.5-6, not in this clause. The same exponential form also governs cluster-specific random variables under the spatial consistency procedure of §7.6.3. See `7.4-pathloss.yaml` under `shadow_fading_autocorrelation` for the machine-readable form.
